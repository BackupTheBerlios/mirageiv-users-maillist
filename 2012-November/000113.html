On Mon, Nov 12, 2012 at 6:44 PM, jan <vereecke.jan at skynet.be> wrote:
> In case you start a screenshow with a very large amount of images (think >
> 10000), the screenshow starts already while the image_list is being
> populated I the background. At screenshow start, randomlist is initialized,
> and gets the size of image_list of that moment. With large amounts of
> pictures, this size is lower than the final list.
>
> When the next image needs to be selected, image_list has increased and is
> now larger than randomlist.
>
> Under these conditions, the statement
>
> j = random.randint(0, len(self.image_list)-1)
>
> can return an index that is out of bounds for the randomlist. => the
> screenshow blocks
>
> My suggested solution in the patch: grow randomlist as needed.
>
Thanks for the report
I fixed this in svn rev 377 in an alternative way with list.extend()

> The second problem: with very large screenshow lists, when most pictures
> have already been shown (example: 9999 out of 10000 are shown),
>
> The code
>
> while self.randomlist[j]:
>
> j = random.randint(0, len(self.image_list)-1)
>
> May take an almost infinite time to find that one remaining image.
>
> My suggested solution: if the first random location is already shown, simply
> iterate the list until you find the next one not shown.

I fixed this in svn trunk a while ago
